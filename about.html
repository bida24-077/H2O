About Water

Explore the science, importance, and conservation of water

Why Water is Important

Water is essential for all known forms of life. It makes up about 60% of the human body and is crucial for digestion, temperature regulation, and cellular function. Plants need water for photosynthesis, and animals rely on it for hydration and habitat.

The Water Cycle

The water cycle describes how water moves through the environment. It includes processes like evaporation, condensation, precipitation, and collection. This cycle helps distribute water across the planet and supports ecosystems.

Water Conservation

Conserving water helps ensure a sustainable future. Simple actions like fixing leaks, using water-efficient appliances, and reducing outdoor water use can make a big difference. Education and awareness are key to protecting this vital resource.
